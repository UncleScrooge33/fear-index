Privacy Policy

Last updated:

World Feeling measures the global fear/hope sentiment through anonymous votes. This page explains what data we collect and how it is used.

1. Data we collect

  • Votes: When you submit a vote (0–100), we store the numeric value and submission timestamp to compute aggregated statistics. We do not ask you to create an account.
  • Log/technical data: Our hosting provider may collect standard server logs (IP address, user-agent, date/time) for security and debugging.
  • Advertising cookies (when enabled): We plan to use Google AdSense to display ads. AdSense may set/read cookies or similar identifiers to measure ad performance and deliver relevant ads.

2. How we use the data

  • Compute and display aggregated sentiment charts.
  • Ensure site security and performance (logs).
  • Show and measure advertising (AdSense), subject to your consent where required.

3. Legal basis

  • Votes & logs: Legitimate interest (provide the service, prevent abuse).
  • Advertising cookies: Consent (in the EU/EEA). Ads do not set non-essential cookies until you consent via our cookie banner.

4. Sharing

We share data with service providers strictly to operate the site (e.g., hosting, analytics/ads). When AdSense is enabled, Google acts as an independent controller for advertising data.

5. Data retention

  • Votes: retained to compute historical charts.
  • Logs: kept for a limited period by the host for security/debugging.
  • Advertising data: governed by Google’s policies.

6. Your choices

  • You can refuse advertising cookies in the EU/EEA (cookie banner) and change your choice at any time.
  • You may contact us to exercise applicable privacy rights (access, deletion, etc.).
